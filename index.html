Bouyancy Simulation

Description

  • You are looking at a bunch of particles bouncing off of each other, transferring energy.
  • One particle is blue. You can alter the properties of the blue particle with the sliders on the left prefixed with "m_"
  • By increasing the “m_size”, you are effectively reducing it’s density
  • You can alter the properties of the red particles with the sliders prefixed with "p_"
  • A warning that many of the combinations of those sliders have pretty extreme results (the scale of many of them is very large and unwieldy to edit via a slider. not to mention adding "mass" via a slider violates some of newton's laws...)

To Play

  1. Watch the blue particle for a bit. Notice how it just bumps along just like any other particle. If anything it might tend to sink: it's mass is 10 times that of the other particles.
  2. Slide the m_size slider all the way to the right. The mass of the blue is still 10 times that of any mass its bumping into but its size is much more than 10 times that of the other mass. That means that it's coming into contact with a lot more particles than before! In fact, it's coming into contact with enough particles that the quantity of "small collisions" with other particles trying to fall down makes up for the fact that each collision is very weak, and as a result, the blue particle "floats!"
