Bouyancy Sim (Teacher's Beta)

This one is admittedly a bit experimental.
The goal was to simulate just the very basics of particle physics: collision/energy transfer, and gravity, and hope that the properties of bouyancy would naturally emerge.
Technically, this was a success. But it deviates from reality just enough to be a bit wonky.

What we have here are a bunch of particles bouncing off of each other.
One particle is blue. You can alter the properties of the blue particle with the sliders on the left prefixed with "m_"
You can alter the properties of the red particles with the sliders prefixed with "p_".
A warning that many of the combinations of those sliders have pretty extreme results (the scale of many of them is very large and unwieldly to edit via a slider. not to mention adding "mass" via a slider violates some of newton's laws...)

I'd recommend doing the following to get a feel for what I was getting at:
1. Hit refresh (start with a new, untouched game)
2. Watch the blue particle for a bit.
- Notice how it just bumps along just like any other particle.
- If it gets stuck to an edge, feel free to reset again.
- If anything it might tend to sink: it's mass is 10 times that of the other particles.
- - this means that when there is a collision, the other particle gets affected 10 times more severely than it does.
- - since gravity is pulling everything down, everything wants to, well, go down.
- - but since the blue particle is so much more massive, any bumping along the way only effects the blue particle a little bit- not enough to prevent it from sinking
3. Now, slide the m_size slider all the way to the right
- - the mass of the blue is still 10 times that of any mass its bumping into
- - but its size is much more than 10 times that of the other mass
- - that means that it's coming into contact with a lot more particles than before!
- - in fact, it's coming into contact with enough particles that the quantity of "small collisions" with other particles trying to fall down makes up for the fact that each collision is very weak.
- - and as a result, the blue particle "floats"!

Some notes on the sim:
In reality, the number of particles would be on the order of trillions of trillions- not "1000".
The increase in particles playing this "bumping into eachother" game will just serve to even out and smooth the interaction.
Unfortunately, we can't simulate that many particles very easily on a computer.
Because of this, the simulation doesn't work 100% of the time, but hopefully it gets the idea across.
But hey, we simulated bouyancy without needing to use any bouyancy equations! That's pretty cool!

Now that you have a feel for this, feel free to play around, but be wary that things can get wonky fast.
